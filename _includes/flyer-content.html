A workshop on how people use, experience, and are affected by generative AI and computer vision systems.

When: June 3 or June 4, 2026 (TBD)

Where: Denver, Colorado

About the Workshop

Humans of Generative AI brings together work on how people use, experience, and are affected by generative AI and computer vision systems. The workshop connects human-centered findings to the design, evaluation, and deployment of AI systems.

Call for Participation

We are seeking poster submissions that connect real-world user insights to technical AI design, evaluation, and deployment. Researchers and practitioners whose work connects user insights to technical AI requirements are encouraged to submit a title, 200-300 word abstract, 1-3 page extended abstract, and short practical relevance statement. Accepted submissions include a poster and a 3-5 minute lightning talk.

We Especially Welcome

  • Social science findings that should shape the technical design of AI systems
  • Technical designs that address unmet user needs or real-world harms
  • Evaluation methods grounded in how people actually use and experience AI systems
  • Interdisciplinary work spanning computer vision, machine learning, HCI, safety, security, ethics, and social science
